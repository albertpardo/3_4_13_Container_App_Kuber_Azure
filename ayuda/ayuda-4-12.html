    • Yikes. My bad.

      Why can't you connect to the mongodb from docker?

      You have your db connection stored in the local.settings.json.

      Something like this:

      1
      "MyDbConnection": "mongodb://cosmos-561123:O42TGv7Rl1L9sdFPZlbNeFF57qmmftDphFO0aC7unhU7Y4bY620symxqfrsmZ5ftDPnNzGDCvcwHFasu7gTaQA==@cosmos-561123.mongo.cosmos.azure.com:10255/?ssl=true&replicaSet=globaldb&retrywrites=false&maxIdleTimeMS=120000&appName=@cosmos-561123@"

      That one doesn't get pushed to your docker image.

      Solution:

      Add it to your Dockerfile as an environment variable:

      1
      ENV MyDbConnection=mongodb://cosmos-561123:O42TGv7Rl1L9sdFPZlbNeFF57qmmftDphFO0aC7unhU7Y4bY620symxqfrsmZ5ftDPnNzGDCvcwHFasu7gTaQA==@cosmos-561123.mongo.cosmos.azure.com:10255/?ssl=true&replicaSet=globaldb&retrywrites=false&maxIdleTimeMS=120000&appName=@cosmos-561123@

      See my Dockerfile as a reference.
